What makes it different

Not a chatbot.
An interviewer.

01
Resume-aware questions

The AI reads your CV before the interview. It asks about your specific projects, internships, and gaps — not generic questions.

02
Voice both ways

The interviewer speaks to you. You answer out loud. This is how real interviews work — train the actual skill, not the typing skill.

03
Off-script moments

Every 3-4 questions, something unexpected. A personal question, a scenario, a challenge. You can't rehearse your way through this.

04
Real pushback

"I'm not convinced — can you give me a concrete example?" Weak answers get challenged. Just like the real thing.

Sample conversation
"I see you interned at Gammon India. Walk me through a specific technical challenge you faced on site — not what the project was, what you personally had to solve."
We had a foundation settlement issue in Block C. I ran the soil bearing capacity calculations and recommended a raft foundation instead of isolated footings...
"I'm not fully convinced — what IS code did you reference for that calculation? And what was the actual bearing capacity value you calculated?"
